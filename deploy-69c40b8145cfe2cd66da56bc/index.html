Overview

Farm-level action. Landscape-scale results.

BBCI connects individual farm stewardship with corridor-scale science and locally led action, making conservation legible at every scale and useful to farmers, researchers, communities, and funders alike.

"We connect farm-level stewardship with corridor-scale science so communities can align conservation where it works best."

On-farm value

  • Support for buffers, wetlands, prairie strips, and perennial cover
  • Focus on acres that are often difficult or less profitable to farm
  • Reduced erosion and stronger long-term resilience

Verified outcomes

  • Field-based monitoring of biodiversity and ecosystem indicators
  • Evidence of what is actually changing on the ground
  • Credible data for adaptive learning and future scaling

Shared benefit

  • Corridor-scale analysis across connected farms
  • Better alignment for public, private, and community investment
  • Working-lands conservation shaped with local partners

How It Works

Simple to participate in. Serious about results.

01

On-farm habitat and profitability

Support for habitat features placed on acres that are often hard to farm, helping improve field performance while keeping the land working.

02

Outcome-verified monitoring

BBCI tracks what is growing and thriving on the ground, not just what was planted.

03

Corridor-scale analysis

Spatial modeling shows how farms function together as connected habitat systems across the landscape.

04

Farmer and community-led learning

Farmers, researchers, and conservation partners interpret results together and keep improving what effective stewardship looks like.

What We Measure

Science-based indicators. Practical on the ground.

BBCI tracks indicators that show how working lands contribute to biodiversity, ecosystem services, and landscape connectivity from enrolled acres to entire corridors.

Habitat and wildlife

  • Habitat quality for birds and pollinators
  • Wildlife presence supported by perennial cover
  • Soil and water indicators linked to runoff reduction

Corridor connectivity

  • How habitat links fragmented natural areas
  • Early signals of corridor-scale ecological function
  • Spatial modeling of farm-to-farm linkages

Scalability and evidence

  • Whether monitoring is affordable and repeatable
  • Whether farm results combine into corridor evidence
  • Whether data can support public-private alignment

Built On Science

Principles that keep the work honest.

i

Farmers and producer networks help shape how the pilot works from day one.

ii

Researchers and technical partners guide indicators and methods independently.

iii

Monitoring stays separate from payment decisions to protect credibility and trust.

iv

Results are shared in aggregate to support voluntary, community-driven alignment.

v

Communities can adapt and lead the model over time as the evidence base grows.
